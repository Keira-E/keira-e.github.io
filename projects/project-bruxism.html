Project Overview

This project involves the design and prototyping of an ultra-compact, minimally invasive biomedical device for monitoring bruxism (teeth grinding). Resembling a modern earplug, the device utilizes highly sensitive pressure transducers to detect masseter muscle activation, a key biomarker for bruxism events. The goal is to create a comfortable, discreet, and accurate tool for long-term clinical and personal health monitoring.

Key Features & Technologies

  • Biomedical Device Prototyping
  • Pressure Transducer Integration
  • Miniaturized Electronics
  • Signal Acquisition & Processing
  • Physiological Monitoring
  • Ergonomic & Discreet Design
